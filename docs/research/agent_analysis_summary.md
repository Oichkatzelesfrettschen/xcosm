- LISA will enable Galactic-scale population studies of SN Ia progenitors

#### Cosmic Explorer (CE)

**Timeline:**
- **Operations start:** Between 2035 and 2040
- NSF Mathematical and Physical Sciences Subcommittee recommends adoption
- LIGO facilities to phase out when CE comes online

**Design:**
- Two facilities in the United States
- **Facility 1:** 40 km arm length
- **Facility 2:** 20 km arm length
- L-shaped surface detectors on flat, seismically quiet land
- 1-meter diameter ultrahigh-vacuum beam tubes

**Cost Estimates:**
- 40 km observatory: ~$1 billion
- 20 km observatory: ~$0.7 billion

#### Global Next-Generation Network Vision
- **Recommended network:** 2× Cosmic Explorer (US) + Einstein Telescope (Europe) + LIGO India
- **Sensitivity improvement:** Up to 8× over current detectors
- Will resolve **all stellar-mass BBH mergers** to edge of observable Universe
- This will effectively remove stochastic BBH component, enabling detection of weaker signals from:
  - Binary neutron stars
  - Core-collapse supernovae
  - Very early Universe processes

#### Core-Collapse Supernova Science with 3G Detectors
- Next Galactic CCSN will be "one of the most interesting astronomical events of the century"
- 3G detectors will directly probe explosion engines
- Can determine neutrino-driven vs. magnetorotational explosion mechanisms
- Can detect CCSN throughout our Galaxy and potentially in nearby galaxies
